*Last updated: May 2026*

Your privacy matters here. This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and what rights you have. We've written it in plain language because legal jargon wastes spoons.

---

## Who we are

No Spoons Wasted is a blog and newsletter for fellow spoonies and those who struggle with low-energy living whether due to illness or some other reason. You can reach us via our [contact form](/contact/) with any questions about this policy.

---

## What we collect and why

### Email address
If you sign up for the No Spoons Wasted newsletter, we collect your email address. We use it to send you a weekly newsletter with recent blog posts, easy recipes, products that save you spoons, and other resources to support the spoonie community. That's it. We don't sell it, share it, or use it for anything else.

Your email is stored and managed through MailerLite. You can read their [privacy policy](https://www.mailerlite.com/privacy-policy){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" aria-label="MailerLite privacy policy (opens in new tab)"}.

You can unsubscribe at any time using the link at the bottom of every email. We won't take it personally.

### Analytics
This site uses Matomo Analytics, a privacy-friendly analytics platform. Matomo is self-hosted, which means your data stays on our server and is never shared with third parties.

Matomo collects basic information about your visit — pages viewed, time on site, approximate location (country/region level), browser type, and device type. This helps us understand what content is useful so we can make more of it. It cannot in any way be used to identify you personally. It has been completely anonymized. 

We have configured Matomo to run in cookieless mode, which means no tracking cookies are set and no personal information is collected through analytics. Because of this, we do not display a cookie consent banner — there is nothing to consent to.

Matomo does not track you across other websites. We do not use Google Analytics or any other third-party tracking service.

### Contact form
If you use our contact form, we collect your name and email address in order to respond to you, and any other information you voluntarily provide to us. We don't add you to our mailing list when you contact us. Joining the mailing list is up to you entirely and can be done on several pages of the site including the home page.

Contact form submissions are processed through Formspree. They do not sell your data and retain it only as long as necessary. You can read their [privacy policy](https://formspree.io/legal/privacy-policy){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" aria-label="Formspree privacy policy (opens in new tab)"}.

### Cookies
We do not use tracking or analytics cookies. Matomo, our analytics platform, is configured to track visits without setting any cookies.

We do not use advertising cookies or third-party tracking cookies. No cookie consent banner is required or displayed on this site.

## Affiliate links

This site and the No Spoons Wasted newsletter contain affiliate links, primarily through the Amazon Associates program. If you click a link and make a purchase, we may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you.

We use this rather small revenue to make the site self-sufficient. It pays for the infrastructure that runs the site, for the tools needed to develop high-quality content for you, and when possible to pay guest bloggers for their posts.

We only recommend products we genuinely believe are useful for our community. We feel this business model is beneficial for everyone. It helps us support our community with quality content with no added cost to them and without the use of predatory marketing or business practices.

## Who we share your data with

We share your data with no one, with the following limited exceptions:

- **MailerLite** — stores your email to send you a weekly newsletter. They are GDPR compliant.
- **Formspree** — processes contact form submissions. They do not sell data and are GDPR compliant.
- **SiteGround** — hosts this website. Any information you submit through this site (such as contact forms) passes through their servers.
- **Legal requirements** — we may disclose information if required by law.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. Ever.

## Your rights

You have rights that we acknowledge and protect! 

- **Access** the personal information we hold about you (email address)
- **Correct** any inaccurate information
- **Delete** your information. Use our [contact form](/contact/) and we will delete your email from MailerLite.
- **Unsubscribe** from our newsletter at any time using the link in any email. Please keep in mind MailerLite doesn't delete your email if you unsubscribe through the system. It sets your status as inactive. To be fully deleted, please use our [contact form](/contact/) and we will delete your email from MailerLite.
- **Opt out of Matomo analytics** — because we use cookieless tracking, no personal data is collected through analytics. If you'd prefer not to be counted at all, you can enable Do Not Track in your browser settings because Matomo respects this setting. A quick search with the search phrase "Turn on Do Not Track on [your browser's name]" will help you do this. 

To exercise any of these rights, use our [contact form](/contact/). We will respond within 5 business days. That timeframe is set because we have to manage our spoons too.

## Data retention

We keep your email address for as long as you are subscribed to our newsletter. When you unsubscribe, MailerLite marks you as inactive in our list. You can always request complete deletion at any time. Please review the "Your Rights" section on this page for more information about how to do that.

Contact form submissions are kept for 30 days only. After 30 days, Formspree will automatically delete anything you submitted through the contact form. This also means we can only reference emails you sent us within the last 30 days and will not have access to correspondence with you longer than that.

This site is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please use our [contact form](/contact/) and we will delete it.
