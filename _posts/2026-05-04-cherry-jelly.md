## Why I Use Psyllium Fiber As My Gel Base

The magic is in the Psyllium fiber, which is a soluble fiber with lots of viscosity. It slows down the release of glucose into your bloodstream and keeps it stable all the way through digestion. This lessens glucose spikes that can cause spoonie crashes. 

Most jelly is made with pectin, which is also a soluble fiber but not really a match for psyllium. Psyllium gives you a thicker gel for a bigger fiber hit and has stronger blood-sugar research behind it. 

> - [Psyllium fiber improves glycemic control proportional to loss of glycemic control](https://ajcn.nutrition.org/article/S0002-9165(23)27235-4/fulltext)
> - [The Effects of Soluble Dietary Fibers on Glycemic Response](https://www.mdpi.com/2304-8158/11/23/3934)

## Choosing Your Fruit Juice for the Jelly

You can use any fruit juice you want. I use cherry because I like the flavor and it's hard to find in the store. What's more important than the flavor is the other ingredients that come along with it. For optimal results:

- Avoid high fructose corn syrup and other added sugars
- Pick a berry juice if you want the added benefits of antioxidants

Tart Cherry is naturally high in anthocyanins, flavonoids, and melatonin. Research has shown this can ease inflammation, help with muscle recovery, and support sleep. I always feel good about those added benefits. I'm also interested in trying it with cranberry juice. 

If you like things a little tangier, you can add a squeeze of lemon in there. I tend to sprinkle in True Lemon lemon crystals because they are easier to keep around than fresh lemons.

## Adjusting the Consistency
Another benefit of making it at home is adjusting the consistency. The thicker the consistency, the more soluble fiber. If you need more glucose control, consider experimenting with a thicker texture. My first attempt was a little soupy, so I had to adjust the recipe for a firmer texture.
