After a long day moving that involved summer heat, stairs, lifting, bending, carrying, I sat on the couch in a full flare. Even after decades of being a spoonie, it always takes me by surprise. So I pulled up my weighted blanket and felt the relief of the pressure sinking in. 

Like many spoonies, I have a strong need to understand how my body works, so I did a little research to understand the power of weighted blankets. Yes, the relief you feel is real. Yes, it has been studied by medical researchers. It doesn't actually help the pain your body is in, but it does help how intense the pain feels. If you have anxiety on top of pain, it helps even more. 

Research shows the weight of the blanket matters, with 15 lbs. being the most helpful. But, weighted blankets are not for every health condition. 

## Why weighted blankets get a bad wrap 
For the longest time, I resisted weighted blankets because I struggled with how to incorporate them into my care routine. They present spoonies with unique challenges.

- **The weight**. They are heavy and hard to lug around. If you are already in pain or have no spoons left for the day, it can feel impossible to drag it from couch to bed or wherever you rest. 

- **The heat**. They can trap in heat and allow it to build up. Heat intolerance is part of my condition, so sleeping with it is off limits. 

- **The immobilization**. Flares bring fatigue and the weighted blanket can almost immobilize you. Even on days it feels good, it can make it hard to reach for a glass of water, not to mention jumping up to go pee on a moment's notice.

## How research got me to give weighted blankets a second chance
If you are a spoonie who has widespread pain that gets worse from stress and anxiety, research shows that weighted blankets have some real potential for you. I know it helps me, and now I know why.

An article by [Baumgartner et. al](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34425251/), published in The Journal of Pain, studied 94 adults using weighted blankets for one week. Here's what they found:

- Weighted blankets helped how chronic pain felt.
- A 15-lb blanket helped more than a 5-lb one.
- It helped people with high anxiety the most.

It's important to know that it isn't reducing your pain in a biological way. There are so many "magic solutions" out there that get our hopes up and then disappoint. But weighted blankets can genuinely reduce the perception of pain. It may not be a cure, and we should be honest about our expectations, but it is a tool that can help on the bad days. 

As spoonies, we will take anything that makes it easier to cope with our conditions.

## The 7-lb lap blanket for daily usage

I have a weighted lap blanket and I love the versatility. I can use it on my lap when sitting upright at my desk, or on my chest when I'm in my recliner. I can sleep with it without getting too hot. Or, I can drape it over my shoulders when I have neck and back tension. I can use it at work, at home, in the car. I can even travel with it. 


## The 15-lb full blanket for the bad days

After reading the research that 15lbs is most effective, I decided to rethink the heavier blankets. Having a powerful tool for the bad days always appeals to me, but it takes some consideration. I have to find ways to work around the limitations so I can get the benefits without the hassle. 

> As spoonies, we are often problem solving things other people don't ever have to worry about. And that's ok. It's what makes us stronger.

Here are the things I had to think through to create a setup that worked for me:.

- I knew it would be easier to use in winter when trapping in heat is less of a concern. If I wanted to use it in summer, I needed to pair it with something cooling.

- I wanted to use it for a limited time instead of sleeping with it. So I decided I needed a timer so I didn't drift off and wake up hot and dehydrated.

- I need to set up one therapy zone and keep the heavier blanket in that one location so I didn't have to drag it everywhere. 

## Here's my setup 

On the bad days, when I've overdone it and landed myself in a world of hurt, this is my new set up for reducing how much the pain affects me.

- 15 lb weighted blanket at the end of the bed that I can easily pull up during therapy sessions.
- Set a 20 minute timer on my phone so I don't over do it.
- Use a clip-on bedside fan to keep my temperature under control.
- Pair it with this eye messaging mask to enhance the calming effects.
- Use my favorite app - mindspa - for sound therapy while using it.


## Weighted blankets aren't for everyone

Deep pressure isn't for every health condition. You should always consult your doctor because we share information to help give you leads to follow-up on responsibility. I am not a doctor, and this is not medical advice. 

Here are a few I know for sure shouldn't use weighted blankets but this list is not exhaustive. 

- People with untreated sleep apnea or significant respiratory issues
- Anyone with circulation problems serious enough that compression is a concern
- People with claustrophobia or trauma responses to having movement restricted
- Mobility limitations where you genuinely can't get out from under it on your own

And remember, this helps your perception of pain. So if it doesn't feel good, it's not helping. It's not something you should power through because it is "supposed" to help. Always listen to your body. 

## Bottom line on weighted blankets

Weighted blankets aren't a cure for chronic pain. The research is clear about that. But the pressure can help calm the pain signals and make the bad days a little easier. If you have anxiety, it has a real shot at helping you.
